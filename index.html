Tsunami Historical Data from 1800 to 2021

A tsunami is a series of waves in a water body caused by the displacement of a large volume of water, generally in an ocean or a large lake.

How often do Tsunamis appear?

According to the Global Historical Tsunami Database, it estimates that tsunamis that inflict harm or fatalities close to their source happen approximately twice a year and about twice every decade, a tsunami that damages or kills people on remote coasts strikes.

Where do tsunamis most often occur in the world?

According to the Choropleth Map, the countries that have higher number of tsunami occured in the world are Indonesia, Chile, Japan, and United States of America. Due to the fact that there are so many active undersea earthquake zones along the Pacific Rim, which borders the ocean, tsunamis most frequently strike Indonesia and the Pacific Ocean.

How are Tsunami and Earthquake related?

The Tsunami Intensity is defined by Soloviev and Go (1974) with formula I = log2(2^1/2 * h) where "h" is the average runup height of the wave.

The "size," or amplitude, of seismic waves generated by an earthquake source and recorded by seismographs is referred to as Earthquake Magnitude.

According to the Symbol Proportional Map, tsunami mostly occured when an earthquake with magnitude higher than or equal to 4 happened.

Which country has the highest total deaths caused by Tsunami?

Indonesia has the highest number of deaths. This is due to one of the worst tsunamis ever occured, the 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami. In Sumatra, Indonesia, on December 16, 2004, what is widely regarded as the worst tsunami in history struck. It was caused by a 9.1 magnitude earthquake that occurred off the nation's coast. The earthquake caused a tsunami that was 50 m (164 ft) tall and travelled about five kilometres (three miles) inland. According to the Indonesian Ministry of Health, 131,028 people died and 37,000 people have gone missing as a result of this catastrophic natural disaster.
