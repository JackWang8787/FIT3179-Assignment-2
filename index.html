A tsunami is a series of waves in a water body caused by the displacement of a large volume of water, generally in an ocean or a large lake.

What are tsunami intensity and earthquake magnitude?

The tsunami intensity is defined by Soloviev and Go (1974) with formula I = log2(2^1/2 * h) where "h" is the average runup height of the wave.

The "size," or amplitude, of seismic waves generated by an earthquake source and recorded by seismographs is referred to as earthquake magnitude.

According to the Symbol Proportional Map, tsunami mostly occured when an earthquake with magnitude higher than 4 happened.

Which countries have the highest total deaths caused by tsunami ?

According to the Bubble Plot, the top 10 countries with highest total deaths caused by tsunami are Chile, China, Haiti, Indonesia, Iran, Italy, Japan, Pakistan, Peru, and Turkey. Among them, Indonesia has the highest number of deaths whereas Iran has the lowest number of deaths.
